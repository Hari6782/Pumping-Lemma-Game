The Pumping Game

Challenge the computer's claim that a language is regular by using the pumping lemma to force a contradiction.

Choose an exercise

The computer claims that the language $L$ over the alphabet $\Sigma$ is regular. It also claims it can build a finite automaton accepting $L$ using $n$ states, or a regular grammar defining $L$ with $n-1$ nonterminal symbols.

Your turn: enter a word belonging to $L$ that is at least $n$ characters long and press Continue.

Notes

You can enter shorthand such as a5 for aaaaa, and expressions like (a3b5)2 work too. Characters besides a, b, digits, and parentheses are ignored.

Some of these languages are actually regular, so the computer should win every time. Spotting those cases is part of the game.

Variables like $m$ and $n$ are natural numbers, and $w^{\mathtt{R}}$ means the reverse of $w$.
